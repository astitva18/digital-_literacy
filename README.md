### 📌 Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide *(Module 5)* | 20 marks

**Part A — Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud tricks victims into authorising transactions by exploiting a simple misunderstanding — that entering a UPI PIN is needed to *receive* money, when in fact it only authorises *outgoing* payments.

**How It Happens (Step-by-Step):**
1. Scammer contacts victim via phone/WhatsApp, posing as bank agent or buyer
2. Builds trust by claiming account issues or making a purchase offer
3. Sends a UPI *collect request* and tells victim "enter PIN to receive money"
4. Victim enters PIN — unknowingly authorising an outgoing payment
5. Money transfers instantly and is nearly impossible to recover
6. Scammer blocks the victim and disappears

**Who Is Targeted:** First-time UPI users, online sellers (OLX/Quikr), senior citizens, students targeted with fake job/scholarship offers.

**Consequences:** Financial loss of ₹5,000–₹2,00,000+, psychological harm, irreversible transactions, difficult legal recovery.

*Source: Ministry of Home Affairs, Annual Report on Cybercrime, 2023–24*

**Part B — Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist**

| # | Tip | Category |
|---|-----|----------|
| 1 | Use strong, unique passwords for every account | General Safety |
| 2 | Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all accounts | General Safety |
| 3 | Never click links in unsolicited SMS, WhatsApp, or email | Phishing |
| 4 | Keep apps, phone OS, and antivirus up to date | General Safety |
| 5 | Never share OTPs, PINs, or passwords with anyone | General Safety |
| 6 | Check app permissions regularly and revoke unnecessary access | Privacy |
| 7 | **You never need a PIN to receive UPI money — if asked, it's fraud** | 💸 UPI Safety |
| 8 | **Never scan a QR code sent by a buyer or stranger** | 💸 UPI Safety |
| 9 | Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your banking app | 💸 UPI Safety |
| 10 | Check bank statements weekly for unauthorised transactions | Financial Safety |

**📣 Report Cybercrime:**
- 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- 📞 **Helpline:** **1930** *(call immediately after financial fraud — time is critical)*

**Reflection — What Surprised Me Most (100–150 words):**

> While researching UPI fraud, what surprised me most was that the scam requires absolutely no hacking. Victims willingly hand over their PIN because they genuinely believe they are receiving money. The entire attack is psychological. I had always assumed cybercrime involved complex technical exploits; the reality is that the most damaging frauds exploit a simple gap in understanding — that entering a UPI PIN always means money *leaves* your account, never enters it. As a result, I will personally change one habit: I will never enter my UPI PIN during a phone call or under any pressure, no matter how convincing the caller sounds. I will hang up, breathe, and verify through the official app or bank helpline before taking any action.
